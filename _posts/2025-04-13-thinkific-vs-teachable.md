## Thinkific — What We Liked

### The Free Plan Is Actually Free

Thinkific's free plan lets you:
- Build unlimited courses
- Have unlimited students
- Use 1 custom domain
- Access basic analytics

The only limitation: no paid courses (transaction fees apply if you sell).

For testing your course content before monetizing — this is perfect.

### No Transaction Fees on Paid Plans

Upgrade to Pro ($99/mo) and there are zero transaction fees. Every dollar you earn is yours.

Teachable's 5% transaction fee on their free plan adds up fast. At $10,000 in sales = $500 in fees.

### Cleaner Interface

Thinkific's course builder is more intuitive. Drag-and-drop video lessons, clean quiz builder, easier structure for complex courses.

We built our course in Thinkific in 2 days. Same content took 4 days in Teachable.

---

## Thinkific — What We Didn't Like

### Limited Gamification

Teachable has better:
- Points and leaderboards
- Badges and achievements
- Quizzes with time limits

If you're building a community course with engagement mechanics, Teachable wins here.

### Fewer Native Integrations

Teachable has 100+ native integrations vs. Thinkific's 50+. Not a dealbreaker — both support Zapier — but worth noting.

---

## Teachable — What We Liked

### Checkout Upsells = Higher AOV

Teachable's built-in upsells let you offer:
- Course + coaching bundle
- Upsell to higher tier
- One-click add-ons at checkout

This alone can double your average order value. For affiliate marketers selling courses, this is significant.

### Better Analytics Dashboard

Teachable's reporting is more detailed:
- Revenue by course
- Student progress heat maps
- Completion rates by lesson
- Cohort comparisons

If you're serious about optimizing your course business, Teachable's analytics are stronger.

---

## Teachable — What We Didn't Like

### The 5% Free Plan Fee

Teachable's free plan charges 5% on every sale. That feels predatory for a "free" plan.

Thinkific doesn't charge transaction fees on paid plans. Teachable's model penalizes growth.

### More Complex Interface

Teachable has more features — which means more complexity. The learning curve is steeper.

For non-technical creators, Thinkific is faster to get started.
